HaloCard — Smart Contact QR Card Generator · SETUP & DEPLOY
================================================================
Folder: 9-halocard/
Same stack and one-click Railway deploy as susu and the school:
one self-contained Node service (built-in node:sqlite, no runtime
dependencies). The QR + PDF libraries are vendored locally in
public/vendor, so there are no CDNs and the security policy stays tight.

----------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IT DOES
----------------------------------------------------------------
A personal generator for your Halo brands. You make contact cards
that show only Name + Logo + QR on the printed card. The QR is
DYNAMIC: it points to /c/<slug>, a live profile page with a
"Save to contacts" button (a vCard). Because it's dynamic, you can
edit a card's phone, email, address, etc. anytime and the SAME printed
QR keeps working — no reprinting. It also counts scans and saves.

Features:
  • Full contact form (name, title, company, phones, email, website,
    address, socials, profile photo, company logo)
  • 5 templates: Corporate, Executive, Minimalist, Premium (black/gold),
    Modern — all in the Halo kente palette, with an accent-colour picker
  • Live front/back preview with the QR rendered in-browser
  • Export PNG, SVG, and print-ready PDF (300 DPI, bleed, crop marks;
    85×55 mm, 90×50 mm, or custom)
  • Dynamic profile page + live vCard ("Save to contacts")
  • Linktree-style profile dashboard: photo, hero banner in the card's
    accent, Save-to-Contacts button, Call/Email quick actions, and
    brand-coloured social chips (WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, TikTok,
    Snapchat, LinkedIn, X, Website) that deep-link straight to each
    profile. Handles are accepted (e.g. @halo.gh) and auto-converted
    to full URLs; full URLs pass through unchanged.
  • Scan + contact-saved tracking, per card and in the dashboard
  • LEAD CAPTURE: every profile has a "Share your details back" form —
    visitor's name/phone/email/note lands in your Leads inbox, with
    one-tap WhatsApp / Call / Email reply buttons and CSV export
  • PAY ME button: add your Paystack payment link in "Smart extras"
    and the profile shows a payment button (money goes straight to
    your own Paystack — the app never holds funds)
  • Per-card analytics: 30-day activity chart + taps on every social
    link (Stats button on each card)
  • Custom links: choose your own slug (e.g. /c/bismark) in Smart
    extras — careful: changing it after printing breaks printed QRs
  • NFC: "Write to NFC card" button (Chrome on Android) writes your
    card URL onto blank NTAG NFC cards — tap-to-share like Popl
  • Email signature generator (Sig button on each card)
  • WhatsApp link previews: shared card links show your photo (og:image)
  • Bulk create from a CSV (columns: name, company, position, phone, email)
  • Enable/disable a card (a disabled QR shows "no longer active")

----------------------------------------------------------------
ENVIRONMENT VARIABLES
----------------------------------------------------------------
Required:
  AUTH_SECRET       long random string (signs your login token)
  NODE_ENV          production   (refuses to boot on a weak secret)
  ADMIN_EMAIL       your login email
  ADMIN_PASSWORD    your login password
  APP_URL           https://YOUR-APP.up.railway.app   (goes into the QR links)
Do NOT set PORT — Railway sets it.
If ADMIN_* are unset on first boot, a login is created and printed once
to the deploy logs.

----------------------------------------------------------------
DEPLOY ON RAILWAY (same as your other apps)
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Push the 9-halocard folder to GitHub (its own repo, or as a
   subfolder — then set Root Directory to 9-halocard in Railway).
2. Railway -> add service -> Deploy from the repo. It auto-detects the
   Dockerfile (Node 22). No build command needed.
3. Attach a Volume (Settings -> Volumes), mount path /data. Railway sets
   RAILWAY_VOLUME_MOUNT_PATH automatically and the app stores
   halocard.db there. Skip this and your cards reset on every redeploy.
4. Add the environment variables above.
5. Open https://YOUR-APP.up.railway.app/health -> expect {"ok":true}.
6. Set APP_URL to the real URL (it's used inside every QR), redeploy.

----------------------------------------------------------------
FIRST RUN
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Open "/" and sign in with ADMIN_EMAIL / ADMIN_PASSWORD.
2. "+ New card" -> fill in details, pick a template + accent, upload a
   logo/photo -> Save. Saving generates the live QR link.
3. The card preview shows the QR. Export PNG/SVG/PDF to print.
4. Scan the QR with a phone -> it opens the profile page -> tap
   "Save to contacts" to import the vCard.
5. Later, edit the card (new number, new company) -> the same QR still
   works and now serves the updated details.

----------------------------------------------------------------
NOTES & LIMITS (honest)
----------------------------------------------------------------
• Dynamic vs static QR: dynamic QR (used here) means scanning opens the
  profile page, then one tap saves the contact — this is what makes
  "edit later" and scan-tracking possible. A pure static vCard QR would
  import in one step but could never be edited or tracked. You chose
  dynamic.
• Scan/contact-saved counts work because every scan loads /c/<slug> on
  the server. They require the app to be online (it is).
• Images are stored inside the card record (kept small automatically on
  upload). There's no separate file store / S3 — by design, to match the
  rest of your stack and keep deployment identical.
• PNG/SVG/PDF export happens in your browser (the vendored QR + PDF libs).
  Fonts in exports use a standard sans-serif for consistent rendering.

----------------------------------------------------------------
SECURITY (same hardening as your other apps)
----------------------------------------------------------------
JWT login (scrypt-hashed password, 30-day token), constant-time token
checks, per-IP login rate limiting, request body cap, security headers
(CSP, HSTS, nosniff, frame-deny), CORS locked (no wildcard), and a
production guard that refuses a weak AUTH_SECRET. Set a strong
AUTH_SECRET and a real ADMIN_PASSWORD before going live.
